# Length and Budgeting

When space is limited, optimize for issue coverage and answer strength, not polish.

## Default Budget Order

1. Keep direct answers to the highest-risk concerns.
2. Keep the strongest supporting evidence.
3. Keep narrow concessions and scope boundaries.
4. Keep support-maintaining replies to positive reviewers.
5. Trim repeated gratitude, background, and soft transitions first.

Do not allocate space evenly by reviewer if the score risk is uneven. Spend more words where the score can still move.

## Compression Rules

- Replace long scene-setting with one sentence.
- Merge repeated concerns across reviewers in the opener only if doing so does not obscure reviewer-specific answers.
- Prefer one sharp example over three weak ones.
- If a point is unsupported, shorten it instead of padding it.
- Do not cut the sentence that actually answers the question.

## When to Use a Global Opener

Use a short opener when several reviewers share the same themes, such as:

- missing related work
- assumption realism
- theorem clarity
- extra comparison requests

Do not let the opener replace reviewer-specific answers.

If the venue format or AC discussion benefits from it, let that opener function as a one- or two-sentence synthesis of the paper-level clarification.

Keep that synthesis short. If it starts absorbing reviewer-specific blockers, move the substance back into local replies.
Do not spend the opener on a weakness inventory or revision plan. Use it to state what is clarified and what still stands.

## When Thread Format Is Better

If the venue discussion is already thread-based or follow-up heavy, answer per thread and keep cross-thread synthesis short.
